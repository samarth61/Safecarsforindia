WHAT IS GLOBAL NCAP?

Understanding NCAP and its importance:-

The New Car Assessment Program or NCAP was born in the USA in 1978. Before getting into the specifics of grading a vehicle on its safety, you must know how such tests are performed. The Global NCAP test looks to simulate the head-on collision between two cars. The vehicle to be tested maintains a speed of 64 km per hour and is driven into a deformable wall, which is similar in weight to the tested model. However, Indian authorities follow a slightly different regulation when it comes to automobile crash testing. Rather than maintaining a 64kmph speed, the front offset test in India is restricted to a speed of just 56kmph, which means some cars may get ranked high in safety as per the Indian Government’s safety testing but still fail to grade high in the NCAP test

What are the different scores under NCAP?

NCAP scale is divided into star ratings, i.e., the safest vehicles are awarded 5 stars, while unsafe vehicles are generally scored lower. Two specific categories are considered when rating a car’s safety:

  • -Adult Occupant Protection
  • -Child Occupant Protection
